YAHTZEE

Objective:

The goal of Yahtzee is to score the highest total points by rolling five dice and filling out the scorecard with different combinations of dice rolls. Each player has 13 turns to roll the dice and try to achieve these combinations.

Gameplay:

Players take turns rolling the dice. On each turn, the player gets up to three rolls of the dice.

First roll:

The player rolls all five dice.

Second roll:

After the first roll, the player can choose to keep some dice by clicking on them and re-roll others. Up to five dice can be rolled, and any number can be kept.

Third roll:

The player may again choose to keep some dice by clicking on them and re-roll the others. After the third roll, the player must choose a category on the scorecard to record their score based on the dice rolled.

Categories:

There are 13 categories on the scorecard, split into two sections:

Left Section:

Ones:

Add up all the dice showing the number 1.

Twos:

Add up all the dice showing the number 2.

Threes:

Add up all the dice showing the number 3.

Fours:

Add up all the dice showing the number 4.

Fives:

Add up all the dice showing the number 5.

Sixes:

Add up all the dice showing the number 6.

Bonus for Upper Section:

If the total score in the upper section is 63 or higher, the player earns a bonus of 35 points.

Right Section:

Three of a Kind:

At least three dice with the same number. Score the total of all five dice.

Four of a Kind:

At least four dice with the same number. Score the total of all five dice.

Full House:

A set of three of one number and two of another number. Score 25 points.

Small Straight:

Four consecutive numbers (e.g., 1-2-3-4). Score 30 points.

Large Straight:

Five consecutive numbers (e.g., 1-2-3-4-5). Score 40 points.

Yahtzee:

Five of the same number. Score 50 points.

Chance:

Any combination of dice. Score the total of all five dice.

Scoring:

After each turn, the player chooses one category to score based on their roll. The score is recorded on the scorecard, and that category is "locked out" for the rest of the game. If the player cannot or chooses not to score for a particular category, they must mark it with a 0.

End of the Game:

The game ends when all players have filled in all 13 categories on their scorecard. Players then total their scores in each section (Left and Right).

Winner:

The player with the highest total score at the end of the game wins.
